Sensorized Test Fixture

A custom fixture integrating force sensors and optical encoders to validate component tolerances during assembly. Built to catch defects early in the production line.

What I built

A modular fixture frame with embedded load cells and rotary encoders, connected to a Raspberry Pi running a Python data acquisition script. The system logs force-displacement curves in real-time and flags outliers based on statistical thresholds.

How it was validated

Ran 200+ test cycles with known-good and known-bad parts. Achieved 98% detection rate for out-of-spec components with zero false positives. Production team confirmed a 40% reduction in downstream rework.

Week 1:Sketched initial concepts and consulted with assembly techs to understand pain points. Learned that visual inspection was missing micro-cracks in plastic housings.

Week 2:Prototyped a simple jig using off-the-shelf aluminum extrusion and 3D-printed sensor mounts. First tests showed promising correlation between force spikes and defects.

Week 3:Integrated encoders to capture angular position during insertion. Discovered that defects often occurred at specific rotation angles—this became a key detection parameter.

Week 4:Rewrote the data pipeline to handle real-time streaming and added a simple LED indicator for pass/fail. Techs loved the instant feedback.

Week 5:Ran validation trials with production parts. Tuned thresholds based on statistical analysis of the dataset. Documented calibration procedure for future maintenance.

Week 6:Deployed to production floor. Collected feedback and made minor ergonomic adjustments to fixture height and cable routing.

Compact Power & Controls Unit

A custom enclosure housing power supplies, motor drivers, and safety interlocks for a robotic arm prototype. Designed for easy access during testing while meeting UL safety requirements.

What I built

A laser-cut aluminum chassis with modular DIN rail sections. Integrated emergency stop circuit, current monitoring, and thermal cutoffs. All wiring follows IEC color codes and includes labeled terminal blocks for serviceability.

How it was validated

Conducted thermal testing under max load for 8 hours—temps stayed within spec. Safety interlocks triggered correctly in all failure modes. Third-party electrical inspector reviewed and approved for lab use.

Week 1:Mapped out power requirements for all subsystems. Created a single-line diagram showing power flow and protection devices. Consulted UL 508A standard for industrial control panels.

Week 2:Designed enclosure in CAD with focus on airflow and cable management. Used thermal simulation to verify cooling strategy—passive convection was sufficient, no fans needed.

Week 3:Sourced components from reputable suppliers with proper certifications. Laser-cut prototype enclosure and assembled first rev. Discovered cable routing conflicts—revised panel layout.

Week 4:Implemented safety interlocks using dual-channel relays. Tested all failure modes: e-stop, overtemp, overcurrent. System shut down safely in every scenario.

Week 5:Documented wiring diagrams and created a troubleshooting guide. Added test points for voltage measurements. Ran extended thermal soak test—no issues.

Week 6:Final inspection by electrical engineer. Made minor labeling improvements. Unit passed all safety checks and was cleared for lab deployment.

Automated Test Rig with Data Logging

A pneumatic test rig that cycles components through 10,000 operations while logging pressure, temperature, and cycle time. Built to validate durability claims before product launch.

What I built

A frame-mounted pneumatic actuator controlled by a PLC, with pressure transducers and thermocouples feeding into a data acquisition system. LabVIEW script logs all parameters to CSV and displays real-time plots. Pass/fail criteria are configurable via a simple config file.

How it was validated

Ran initial tests with known-good samples to establish baseline performance. Then tested pre-damaged parts to verify the rig could detect failures. Achieved 100% detection of failure modes. Endurance tests ran unattended for 72+ hours with no issues.

Week 1:Defined test requirements with the product team. Needed to simulate 10 years of use in 2 weeks. Calculated cycle rate and identified critical parameters to monitor.

Week 2:Designed mechanical fixture to hold components securely while allowing full range of motion. Used pneumatic cylinder for consistent actuation force. Added limit switches for position feedback.

Week 3:Programmed PLC ladder logic for cycle control. Integrated sensors and verified signal quality. Initial tests revealed noise on pressure readings—added inline filter to clean up signal.

Week 4:Built LabVIEW dashboard for real-time monitoring. Added automatic data export and email alerts for failures. Tested notification system by intentionally triggering fault conditions.

Week 5:Ran first full endurance test. Discovered mechanical wear on fixture after 5,000 cycles—redesigned contact surfaces with hardened inserts. Re-ran test successfully.

Week 6:Documented test procedure and created operator training guide. Delivered rig to QA team with full dataset from validation runs. Product team used data to refine warranty claims.
