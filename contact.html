FAQ

Most projects range from 2-8 weeks depending on scope and complexity. I typically spend the first week on research and prototyping, followed by iterative development cycles with regular check-ins. Rush timelines are possible for smaller scopes, but I always prioritize quality and thorough testing over speed.

I need a clear problem statement, any existing constraints (budget, timeline, materials), desired outcomes, and reference examples if available. Technical specs like load requirements, environmental conditions, or integration points are helpful. Even rough sketches or napkin drawings give me a solid starting point for discussion.

I use CAD software for mechanical design, simulation tools for validation, and version control for all project files. My workflow emphasizes documentation-as-you-go, with regular commits and clear naming conventions. I'm flexible with client-preferred platforms and can adapt to existing toolchains, though I advocate for open formats whenever possible.

Every project includes comprehensive documentation: design rationale, assembly instructions, bill of materials, and test results. I deliver source files in standard formats, annotated drawings, and a project README that explains key decisions. If you need ongoing support or training, I offer post-delivery sessions to ensure smooth handoff.

I typically schedule weekly sync meetings for active projects, with async updates via email or shared docs in between. For rapid prototyping phases, I prefer more frequent touchpoints. I'm responsive during work hours and aim to reply within 24 hours. Transparency is key—I'll flag blockers early and keep you informed of progress.
